# Input VAT Deduction Conditions Reference

## Legal Basis

**Article 14, Law 48/2024/QH15** (effective 01/07/2025)
**Article 14, Circular 219/2013/TT-BTC** (Implementation details)

---

## Overview

Input VAT deduction allows businesses to deduct input VAT paid on purchases from output VAT collected on sales, resulting in net VAT liability. **All four conditions must be satisfied** for input VAT to be deductible.

---

## Four Required Conditions

### Condition 1: Payment Method

**Requirement:** Payment via bank transfer for transactions ≥20,000,000 VND

| Amount | Payment Requirement | Documentation |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| < 20,000,000 VND | No specific method required | Invoice sufficient |
| ≥ 20,000,000 VND | Bank transfer mandatory | Invoice + Bank record |

**Bank Transfer Requirements:**
- Transfer must be from buyer's bank account to seller's bank account
- Transfer date must be on or after invoice date
- Transfer amount must cover the invoice (or documented installments)
- Bank record must show: invoice number, date, parties, amount

**Legal References:**
- Article 14(1), Law 48/2024/QH15
- Article 14, Circular 219/2013/TT-BTC

**Exceptions to Bank Payment Rule:**
| Exception | Conditions |
|-----------|-----------|
| Taxes and state fees | Payment to state treasury via bank or counter |
| Remote areas | No banking services available in the area |
| Emergency situations | Natural disasters, force majeure |
| Other exceptions | As specified by current regulations |

---

### Condition 2: Business Purpose

**Requirement:** Goods/services used for VAT-taxable production or business activities

| Business Activity | VAT Treatment |
|-----------------|--------------|
| VAT-taxable production | Input VAT deductible |
| VAT-taxable business operations | Input VAT deductible |
| VAT-exempt activities | Input VAT generally NOT deductible |
| Non-business activities | Input VAT NOT deductible |
| Personal use | Input VAT NOT deductible |

**Allocation Required When:**
- Goods/services used for both taxable and exempt activities
- Partial business use with mixed purposes
- Input VAT must be allocated proportionally

**Legal References:**
- Article 14(2), Law 48/2024/QH15
- Article 6, Circular 219/2013/TT-BTC

---

### Condition 3: Valid Documentation

**Requirement:** Proper VAT invoice or equivalent supporting document

**VAT Invoice Requirements:**

| Field | Requirement |
|-------|------------|
| Invoice serial number | Registered with tax authority |
| Invoice number | Sequential, not duplicated |
| Seller information | Name, tax code, address |
| Buyer information | Name, tax code, address |
| Description | Clear description of goods/services |
| Quantity | Unit of measurement and quantity |
| Unit price | Price per unit |
| Total amount | Before VAT |
| VAT rate | Applicable rate (0%, 5%, 8%, 10%) |
| VAT amount | Calculated VAT |
| Total including VAT | Gross amount |
| Signature | Authorized signatures |
| Date | Date of invoice issuance |

**Electronic Invoice Requirements (if applicable):**
- Registered with authorized electronic invoice service
- Transmitted to tax authority within deadline
- Valid digital signature

**Invalid Invoice Situations:**
| Situation | Consequence |
|-----------|------------|
| Invoice from suspended taxpayer | NOT deductible |
| Invoice from unregistered business | NOT deductible |
| Falsified invoice | NOT deductible + penalties |
| Invoice with incorrect information | Review and correction required |
| Invoice outside business activities | NOT deductible |

**Legal References:**
- Article 14(3), Law 48/2024/QH15
- Article 15-20, Circular 219/2013/TT-BTC
- Circular 93/2017/TT-BTC (Electronic invoices)

---

### Condition 4: Goods/Services Received

**Requirement:** Goods/services actually delivered/completed and verifiable

**Goods Verification:**
- Physical receipt of goods documented
- Warehouse receipt or delivery note
- Goods inspection/acceptance record
- Inventory receiving report

**Services Verification:**
- Service completion report
- Acceptance protocol signed by both parties
- Progress reports or milestones
- Service delivery documentation

**Fictitious Transaction Indicators:**
| Red Flag | Action |
|----------|--------|
| No delivery documentation | DO NOT deduct |
| Invoice mismatch with delivery | Investigation required |
| Vendor unable to verify delivery | Flag for review |
| Timing inconsistencies | Clarification needed |

**Legal References:**
- Article 14(4), Law 48/2024/QH15
- Article 3, Circular 219/2013/TT-BTC

---

## Verification Checklist

### For Each Transaction ≥20,000,000 VND

- [ ] Bank transfer record exists
- [ ] Transfer amount matches invoice
- [ ] Transfer date ≥ invoice date
- [ ] Transfer between correct parties
- [ ] Bank record shows invoice reference

### For Each Transaction <20,000,000 VND

- [ ] No bank transfer required
- [ ] Invoice is valid and complete
- [ ] All required fields present

### For All Transactions

- [ ] Goods/services for taxable business activities
- [ ] No personal or exempt use mixed in
- [ ] Valid VAT invoice obtained
- [ ] Goods physically received or services completed
- [ ] Delivery documentation available

## Special Situations

### Mixed Use Allocation

When goods/services used for both taxable and exempt purposes:

```
Deductible Input VAT = Total Input VAT × (Taxable Use %)
```

**Example (Hypothetical only):**
- Total input VAT: 10,000,000 VND
- Taxable business use: 70%
- Exempt use: 30%
- Deductible amount: 10,000,000 × 70% = 7,000,000 VND

**Legal Reference:** Article 6, Circular 219/2013/TT-BTC

---

### Prepaid Expenses

| Expense Type | Deductibility |
|--------------|--------------|
| Prepaid rent | Deductible when service received |
| Prepaid insurance | Deductible when coverage period applies |
| Advances for goods | Deductible when goods delivered |

---

### Fixed Assets

Input VAT on fixed assets is deductible when:
- Asset used for VAT-taxable production/business
- All four conditions met
- Depreciation treatment follows accounting rules

**Special Rule:** Input VAT on some fixed assets may need allocation for mixed-use situations.

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Prevention |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Missing bank records | Amount disallowed | Maintain organized banking records |
| Wrong invoice date | Timing mismatch | Verify date sequence |
| No delivery proof | Cannot verify receipt | Require delivery documentation |
| Exempt activities | Wrongful deduction | Verify business purpose |
| Vendor issues | Invalid invoice | Check vendor tax compliance |

---

## Documentation Requirements

**Minimum Documentation for Input VAT Deduction:**

| Document | Purpose | Retention |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| VAT Invoice | Primary deduction document | 10 years |
| Bank Transfer Record | Payment verification (≥20M) | 10 years |
| Purchase Contract | Business purpose evidence | 10 years |
| Delivery Note | Goods receipt proof | 10 years |
| Service Report | Service completion proof | 10 years |
| Goods Inspection Record | Acceptance documentation | 10 years |

---

## Escalation Points

**Refer to tax advisor when:**

1. Transaction involves allocation between taxable/exempt use
2. Business has both taxable and exempt activities
3. Unclear whether good/service qualifies for deduction
4. Vendor has tax compliance issues
5. Transaction structure is complex
6. Multiple conditions appear borderline
